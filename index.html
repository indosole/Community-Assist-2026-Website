Our Mission

Building bridges between people who want to help and kids who need it.

Community Assist is a collective of Indonesian businesses, founded by Indosole and PNNY, raising funds to empower local communities through education, environment and opportunity.

Everything we raise goes to our partners' scholarship programs, helping children stay in school and build a future for themselves and their families.

The collective behind it

Founded by Bali businesses, powered by a foundation.

  • Founding partner

    For over 17 years, Indosole has turned discarded tyres and river-collected sandals into recycled footwear, pioneering a future-forward model while creating jobs and community across Indonesia.

    @indosole
  • Founding partner

    Established in 2014, PNNY is Bali's home-grown collective of seven artists, curating the music events that have helped shape the island's scene, on both a local and international stage.

    @pnny_
  • Scholarship partner

    "Ragam" means diversity. The foundation connects people who want to give with communities who need support, and runs the educational scholarship program that every rupiah you give goes toward.

Stories of Impact

What your support makes possible.

From scholarship to graduate

Chintya

Ni Putu Chintya Pratiwi just graduated in accounting from Universitas Mahasaraswati Denpasar, and two weeks later she had already accepted her first job at an accounting firm.

Covering her education costs freed her family to build their own security. Her mum opened a laundry business that's now thriving, so she no longer has to clean kos to get by. When COVID cut her dad's salary in half, the program's extra help with clothes, school supplies, and a water filter carried them through the hardest stretch.

Today her dad's income has recovered, her mum has a steady business of her own, and Chintya contributes fully from her new job. The family now supports itself and is putting her two younger brothers through school. One scholarship, a whole family's future.

Top of her class

Indah

Indah is the firstborn of two, and a high-schooler at SMA Saraswati Denpasar. She keeps top grades while leading the student council and theatre club, and still finds time to tutor younger children to help her parents earn extra income. She dreams of becoming a tax officer.

Back in the classroom

Wahyu

Thanks to supporters last year, Wahyu received a Ragam Foundation scholarship through Community Assist 2024, and he's back in school, now in 8th grade. With another year of support he'll finish his studies, and that same chance can reach more children across Bali.
